Moo Shu Pork


Description

I always order moo shu pork in Chinese restaurants and wanted to try making it at home using a combination of recipes. You can find Chinese pancakes at Asian grocery stores. Let everyone fill their own pancakes and top with the hoisin sauce mixture — it absolutely makes this dish!

Submitted by Lindsay | Updated on July 14, 2025

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons white wine
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 2 teaspoons cornstarch
  • 1 pound pork tenderloin, cut into thin strips
  • 1 ¾ ounces dried shiitake mushrooms
  • ½ cup sliced green onions
  • 3 tablespoons minced garlic
  • 2 tablespoons peeled and minced fresh ginger
  • 1 head napa cabbage
  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 3 tablespoons white wine
  • ½ teaspoon cornstarch
  • ½ teaspoon white sugar
  • ¼ teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil, plus more if needed
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 2 large eggs, beaten
  • 2 tablespoons white wine
  • 1 cup hoisin sauce
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 16 frozen Chinese pancakes, thawed and warmed

Steps

  1. Place 2 tablespoons soy sauce, 2 tablespoons white wine, 1 teaspoon sesame oil, and 2 teaspoons cornstarch in a bowl. Stir pork into marinade and refrigerate 30–60 minutes.
  2. Soften mushrooms in hot water for 15–20 minutes, drain, pat dry, remove stems, and chop finely. Combine with green onions, garlic, and ginger.
  3. Remove outer leaves from napa cabbage. Tear green leaves from stalks, slice stalks thinly, and keep leaves and stalks separate.
  4. Combine 3 tablespoons soy sauce, 3 tablespoons white wine, ½ teaspoon cornstarch, sugar, and pepper in a small bowl. Set aside.
  5. Heat vegetable oil and 1 teaspoon sesame oil in a wok. Add pork; cook until done and lightly browned. Transfer pork to a bowl.
  6. Pour beaten eggs into wok; cook and stir until scrambled. Stir in mushroom mixture for 1–2 minutes. Add cabbage stalks and cook 1 minute. Add cabbage leaves and 2 tablespoons white wine; cook 1 minute. Add cooked pork and reserved sauce; stir until thickened and hot.
  7. Mix hoisin sauce, 1 tablespoon soy sauce, and 1 teaspoon sesame oil. Spread 1 tablespoon of sauce on each pancake, top with ½ cup pork mixture, roll, and serve.
